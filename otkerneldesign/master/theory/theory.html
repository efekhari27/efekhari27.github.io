, a first goal of the following DoE methods is to explore the input space in a space-filling way (e.g., to build a generic metamodel of ). However, this work will exploit these methods with a specific purpose in mind: to numerically integrate $g$ against the probability density function which relates to a central tendency estimation of an output random variable , resulting from an uncertainty propagation step.

Recently, other sampling methods based on the notions of discrepancy between distributions in a kernel-based functional space were used to approximate integrals. More precisely, one can mention the use of the distance called the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) as a core ingredient of advanced sampling methods such as the Support points by (Mak & Joseph, 2018) and the Kernel herding by (Chen & al., 2010). The MMD is convenient to manipulate since it can simply be expressed using the underlying kernel arbitrarily arbitrary chosen.
